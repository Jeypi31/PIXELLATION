Top 10 Pixel Games in 1980s


  • Pac-Man (1980) - An iconic arcade game where players control Pac-Man through a maze, eating pellets and avoiding ghosts


  • Donkey Kong (1981) - Introduced the character Mario and featured platforming gameplay where players must rescue a damsel in distress from a giant ape.


  • Galaga (1981) - A classic arcade shooter where players control a spaceship to fend off waves of alien invaders.


  • Tetris (1984) - A puzzle game where players arrange falling blocks to complete lines and prevent the screen from filling up.


  • Super Mario Bros. (1985) - Revolutionized the platforming genre with its colorful graphics, tight controls, and memorable level design.


  • The Legend of Zelda (1986) - A pioneering action-adventure game that set the standard for exploration, puzzle-solving, and storytelling in video games.


  • Mega Man (1987) - Known for its challenging gameplay and innovative boss battle mechanics, where players can choose the order in which they tackle levels.


  • Castlevania (1986) - A gothic horror-themed platformer known for its atmospheric graphics, challenging gameplay, and memorable music.


  • Metroid (1986) - Introduced non-linear exploration and backtracking mechanics in a side-scrolling action-adventure game set in a sci-fi world.


  • Street Fighter (1987) - Pioneered the fighting game genre with its diverse roster of characters, special moves, and competitive multiplayer gameplay.

Top 10 Pixel Games in 1990s


  • Super Mario World (1990) - Expanded upon the gameplay of its predecessors with larger levels, new power-ups, and the introduction of Yoshi.


  • The Legend of Zelda: A Link to the Past (1991) - Set the standard for action-adventure games with its intricate world design, engaging story, and challenging puzzles.


  • Final Fantasy VI (1994) - Known for its epic story, memorable characters, and innovative gameplay mechanics in the RPG genre.


  • Chrono Trigger (1995) - Regarded as one of the greatest RPGs of all time, featuring time travel, multiple endings, and a captivating story.


  • Street Fighter II (1991) - Popularized competitive fighting games with its diverse cast of characters, special moves, and intense one-on-one battles.


  • Sonic the Hedgehog 2 (1992) - Introduced Sonic's sidekick, Tails, and featured fast-paced platforming action and colorful pixel art.


  • Metal Gear Solid (1998) - Combined stealth gameplay with cinematic storytelling, setting new standards for the action-adventure genre.


  • EarthBound (1994) - A quirky RPG known for its unique humor, memorable characters, and innovative battle system.


  • Diablo (1996) - Popularized the action RPG genre with its addictive gameplay, randomized dungeons, and online multiplayer.


  • Half-Life (1998) - Revolutionized the first-person shooter genre with its immersive storytelling, realistic environments, and innovative gameplay mechanics.

Top 10 Pixel Games in 2000s


  • Pokémon Gold and Silver (2000) - Introduced new Pokémon, features, and the day/night cycle to the beloved RPG series.


  • The Legend of Zelda: The Wind Waker (2002) - Known for its vibrant cel-shaded graphics, expansive world, and innovative sailing mechanics.


  • Super Smash Bros. Melee (2001) - Expanded upon the original with more characters, stages, and gameplay modes, becoming a staple of multiplayer gaming.


  • Grand Theft Auto: Vice City (2002) - Set in a fictionalized version of Miami in the 1980s, known for its open-world gameplay, memorable characters, and soundtrack.


  • Metroid Fusion (2002) - A side-scrolling action-adventure game that introduced new abilities and expanded the lore of the Metroid series.


  • Castlevania: Aria of Sorrow (2003) - Known for its deep RPG elements, engaging story, and challenging gameplay in the Castlevania series.


  • Advance Wars (2001) - A turn-based strategy game with colorful pixel art, deep gameplay mechanics, and a compelling single-player campaign.


  • Paper Mario: The Thousand-Year Door (2004) - A charming RPG with a unique papercraft aesthetic, engaging story, and entertaining turn-based battles.


  • Cave Story (2004) - Developed by a single person, this indie platformer combines challenging gameplay with a captivating story and charming pixel art.


  • Phoenix Wright: Ace Attorney (2001) - A visual novel adventure game where players take on the role of a defense attorney, solving cases and uncovering the truth.
