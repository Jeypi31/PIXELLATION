Pixel Art

Pixel art is a digital art form where images are created and edited at the pixel level, using small, square blocks of color to form larger compositions. Each pixel represents a single point in the image and can be individually manipulated to create detailed and often retro-style graphics. Pixel art has its roots in early video game graphics, where hardware limitations necessitated the use of small, blocky pixels to represent characters, objects, and environments.



Mosaic Art

Mosaic art is a form of art where small pieces of materials, such as glass, stone, ceramic, or tile, are arranged to create a larger image or pattern. These pieces, called tesserae, are often of various colors and shapes, allowing for intricate designs and detailed compositions.Mosaic art has been practiced for thousands of years and can be found in cultures all around the world, from ancient Roman and Byzantine mosaics to contemporary artworks.






VOXEL

Voxels, short for "volumetric pixels," are the 3D equivalent of pixels in 2D graphics. While pixels represent individual points of color on a 2D surface, voxels represent individual points in a three-dimensional space, typically within a cubic grid.

PIXEL

A pixel, short for "picture element," is the smallest controllable element of a digital image. It is a tiny square or dot that contains a single point of color, brightness, and position within the image grid.
